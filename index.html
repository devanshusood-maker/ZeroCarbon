CCTS: The Bridge India Needs

India's Carbon Credit Trading Scheme is the country's first policy instrument to directly target CO₂ emissions. CEEW's modelling shows that even initial CCTS targets can be met through low or negative-cost abatement — meaning industries save money while cutting emissions. As the scheme scales, it creates the carbon price signal that India's financial system needs to assess transition risk, direct capital, and unlock the $3.5 trillion gap. The EU ETS has generated over $109 billion in auction revenue since 2013. CCTS can become India's equivalent engine for climate finance — but only if entities engage strategically from the start.

CBAM Compliance & Documentation

The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism is fully operational in 2026. Indian MSME exporters face installation-level emission calculations, CBAM-compliant reporting, and the challenge of documenting domestic carbon prices paid under CCTS for deduction claims. We make this manageable.

  • Installation-level embedded emission calculations per EU methodology
  • CBAM-compliant reporting and documentation preparation
  • Domestic carbon price documentation for CBAM deduction claims
  • Ongoing compliance monitoring as CBAM scope expands
  • Export risk assessment across EU-bound product categories
